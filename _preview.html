Who I Am

My name is Leran Zhao (赵乐然). I'm 25, from China, currently living and working in Hong Kong. My life has moved in chapters: growing up in China, four transformative years at UC Berkeley, and now building a life in one of Asia's most dynamic cities.

I'm someone who thinks deeply, feels intensely, and cares about growing into the person I imagined as a kid. I once wrote about learning to tie a tie for the first time and thinking, "I feel like I'm growing into the person I imagined as a child." That still captures me well.

Berkeley

I graduated from UC Berkeley in 2024 with a degree in Legal Studies. But the real education happened outside the classroom. I lived at International House, a residential community of students from 77 countries. I served as a Program Leader, hosted Sunday Suppers, supported the US-China peace talk, and ran Lunar New Year celebrations.

I worked as a barista at Edmonds Cafe partly for the free coffee, partly to cover rent. That job did something unexpected: it made me fall in love with the working world and pushed me away from going straight to grad school. There's a dignity in serving people well, in showing up every morning and making something with your hands.

Berkeley's culture of idealism shaped me. The idea that you can be a serious, ambitious person and still believe in justice, beauty, and community — that's something I carry with me.

Hong Kong

I moved to Hong Kong to join Jane Street. I'm currently working in recruiting. It's a place that rewards clarity of thought and precision — both things I value.

What I Care About

Writing

I want to be a writer and I haven't let go of it. Personal essays about identity, growing up between cultures, the tension between idealism and pragmatism. My best writing happens in Chinese, privately, for no audience.

Music

Classical piano background, deep love for jazz. Miles Davis, Coltrane, late nights at Green Mill in Chicago. I think in albums and soundtracks, the way Murakami writes about music.

Fitness

Weightlifting, half marathons (2:18 PR), HIIT. Fitness is how I stay grounded. Training is the one part of my day where the only variable is effort.

Coffee

Former barista, forever coffee person. I believe how you make someone's morning drink says something about how you treat people.
